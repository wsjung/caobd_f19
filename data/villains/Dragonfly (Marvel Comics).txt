'''Dragonfly''' ('''Veronica Dultry''') is a fictional [[supervillain]]ess appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].

==Publication history==
Dragonfly first appeared in ''[[X-Men]]'' #94-95 (Augustâ€“October 1975), and was created by [[Chris Claremont]] and [[Len Wein]].

==Fictional character biography==
Veronica Dultry is endowed with superhuman powers by [[Maggia (comics)|Maggia]] [[scientist]]s in order to serve the [[Boss (crime)|crime lord]] [[Count Nefaria]] among the second group of [[Ani-Men]]. The Ani-Men take control of the [[NORAD]] base at Mount Valhalla, but are defeated by the [[X-Men]],<ref>''X-Men'' #94-95</ref> and imprisoned in the mutant research complex at [[Muir Island]]. She escapes shortly after when [[Erik the Red (comics)|Erik the Red]] breaks into the complex.<ref>''X-Men'' #104</ref>

She is later abducted by the alien [[Stranger (comics)|Stranger]] to his laboratory world, where she and other abductees are manipulated by the [[Overmind (comics)|Overmind]] into battling [[Quasar (comics)|Quasar]].<ref>''Quasar'' #14-16</ref> After she returns to Earth, she mutates further due to the Stranger's experiments, but [[Ant-Man (Scott Lang)|Ant-Man]] aids her in returning to her normal appearance.<ref>''Iron Man Annual'' #12</ref>

Later, she joins [[Superia]]'s [[Femizons]] and battles [[Captain America]] and [[Paladin (comics)|Paladin]].<ref>''Captain America'' #389-392</ref> Dragonfly and several other former Femizons battle Captain America again during an [[Advanced Idea Mechanics|A.I.M.]] weapons demonstration.<ref>''Captain America'' #411-413</ref> 

Much later, Dragonfly is invited to join the [[Crimson Cowl]]'s [[Masters of Evil]] where she battles the [[Thunderbolts (comics)|Thunderbolts]]. They are defeated and sent to prison.<ref>''Thunderbolts'' #24-25</ref> 

In new battle armor, she is sent to attack [[Underworld (comics)|Jackie Dio]] alongside several other criminals. Dio quickly defeats the villains by blowing up the building they were in.<ref>''Underworld'' #4-5</ref>

During the "Opening Salvo" part of the ''[[Secret Empire (comics)|Secret Empire]]'' storyline, Dragonfly is recruited by [[Helmut Zemo|Baron Helmut Zemo]] to join the Army of Evil.<ref>''Captain America: Steve Rogers'' #16</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Dragonfly can fly and possesses a hypnotic gaze. Her physical appearance has also been altered giving her wings, red skin, white pupils and antennae.
